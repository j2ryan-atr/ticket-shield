I. The Citation Is Facially Defective Due to Invalid Officer Signature
The officer signature field on the citation contains only a numeric identifier ("4789201") rather than an actual signature, whether handwritten or electronic. Under CVC §40500(a), the citing officer "shall" sign the notice to appear. Penal Code §853.9 further requires the citation be signed under penalty of perjury. A numeric badge number does not constitute a signature under the ESIGN Act (15 U.S.C. §7001) or UCC §1-201(37). See also State v. Hernandez (2019), holding that a numeric identifier is insufficient as an officer signature on a traffic citation.
II. The Citation Was Filed Beyond the Statutory Deadline
The citation was filed with the court on day 34 following issuance. CVC §40513 requires filing within 30 days. This untimely filing is grounds for dismissal as a matter of law.
III. The Speed Measurement Equipment Was Not Properly Calibrated
The LIDAR unit used (LTI TruSpeed S, S/N: TS-40921) was last calibrated 387 days prior to the citation date. The manufacturer requires annual calibration. Additionally, no tuning fork verification test was documented for the citing officer's shift, in violation of department policy. The reliability of the speed reading cannot be established.
IV. Speed Trap Defense Under CVC §40802(b)
The traffic and engineering survey for this road segment expired 14 months prior to the citation date. Under CVC §40802(b), enforcement of a speed limit based on an expired survey constitutes a speed trap, and evidence obtained thereby is inadmissible per CVC §40803.
